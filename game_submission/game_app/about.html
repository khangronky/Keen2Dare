The Balancing Act: Why Extremes Are Dangerous

The Golden Rule: Any pillar reaching 0% or 100% results in GAME OVER.

This may seem counterintuitive‚Äîwhy would 100% economy or 100% security be bad? The answer lies in the nature of governance:

  • 100% Economy: Unchecked capitalism leads to corporate tyranny and extreme inequality
  • 0% Economy: Total economic collapse and societal breakdown
  • 100% Environment: Over-regulation strangles all industry, leading to unemployment
  • 0% Environment: Ecological catastrophe makes the land uninhabitable
  • 100% Public Support: Populist policies become unsustainable, leading to backlash
  • 0% Public Support: Revolution and removal from power
  • 100% Security: Authoritarian police state with no freedom
  • 0% Security: Anarchy and lawlessness consume the nation

Your goal is to keep all four pillars balanced between 1-99%. This requires strategic thinking, careful planning, and sometimes making unpopular but necessary decisions.

Game Modes

Unlimited Years Mode

Test your endurance as President. There is no time limit‚Äîonly your ability to maintain balance. Each year consists of 12 monthly decisions (proposals). How many years can you survive? Can you maintain power for decades, or will your presidency crumble within the first term?

Limited Years Mode

Set a specific goal: survive for 5 years, 10 years, or even 50 years. If you successfully maintain balance for your entire term, you achieve victory! This mode is perfect for players who want a clear objective and the satisfaction of completing a defined presidential term.

Strategic Tips for New Presidents

  • Watch the bars constantly: Keep an eye on all four pillars‚Äîdon't focus on just one
  • Think ahead: If economy is at 80%, avoid approving pro-business proposals that push it higher
  • Balance opposing forces: Economy and environment often conflict; support and security may clash
  • Remember impacts: As you swipe, the impact preview shows what each choice will do
  • Don't panic: One extreme choice won't ruin you‚Äîit's the cumulative effect that matters
  • Accept trade-offs: Sometimes you must sacrifice one pillar to save another
  • Learn from failure: Each game over teaches you about cause and effect in governance

Understanding Your Advisors

Throughout the game, proposals come from various government officials and advisors. Each advisor represents a specific sector with their own priorities:

  • Treasury Secretary & Commerce Minister: Focus on economic growth and business
  • Environmental Minister & Conservation Director: Prioritize ecological health
  • Health Minister & Education Secretary: Care about public welfare and support
  • Defense Minister & Intelligence Director: Emphasize national security

Understanding each advisor's perspective helps you predict the consequences of your decisions. A proposal from the Defense Minister will likely boost security but may reduce public support or economic resources.
